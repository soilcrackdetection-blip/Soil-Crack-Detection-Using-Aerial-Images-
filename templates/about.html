Technical Architecture

The Soil Crack Detection Platform is a high-resolution geotechnical monitoring system. It integrates multi-stage deep learning pipelines to transform raw aerial imagery into actionable risk assessments.

1. Computer Vision Segmentation

The core segmentation engine utilizes a **ResNet-UNet** architecture. By leveraging a Residual Network (ResNet) backbone as an encoder, the model extracts multi-scale high-level features while the U-Net symmetric decoder preserves spatial resolution for millimeter-precise boundary mapping.

2. Geometric Feature Extraction

Following pixel-level identification, a specialized regression model estimates physical attributes. This stage performs spatial analysis to calculate **length**, **average width**, and **total displacement area**, providing quantitative metrics essential for geotechnical monitoring.

3. Stability Risk Classification

A **Fully Connected Neural Network (FCNN)** processes encoded geometric vectors to determine the structural severity. The system classifies observations into Low, Moderate, or High risk categories based on learned patterns of soil failure and surface instability.
